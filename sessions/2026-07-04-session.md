## What We Found

- **Spotify's rate limit is a cumulative ceiling, not a per-window rate.** Two escalating penalties (9.8h → 21.7h) at conservative settings (20 req/30s, 5 workers) — lowering the rate only delays the wall. Continuing would mean 1–2 weeks of escalating lockouts.
- **Spotify deprecated `preview_url`** for dev-mode apps on 2024-11-27, so the app's live-preview feature ([app.py:51](app.py)) is likely already returning null and failing silently. Previews are a **Need-to-Have**, so they had to be re-sourced.
- **MusicBrainz core data is CC0** (public-domain, fine for public-facing) with **downloadable dumps and no rate limit**; its artist-credit model maps directly to our "who's on this recording" edge.
- **Live MusicBrainz spikes confirmed the model:** 55/100 of Kendrick's recordings had 2+ credited artists; **Paul McCartney is a degree-1 collaborator** (via Kanye's "All Day") — a connection the Spotify crawl never found.
- **Structural gotchas surfaced and were designed around:** one artist has many display names (Ye == Kanye West → must key on MBID); recording-version sprawl (dedup to canonical song); band vs. member is a real distinction.
- **The Beatles are effectively isolated** in a co-credit graph (~0 co-credited recordings on a page of 100) — so under the user's rule they correctly return "no connection," and are NOT reachable via McCartney.

## Decisions Made

- **Pivot the graph source from Spotify to MusicBrainz.** Spotify's escalating penalties make a complete crawl impractical; MusicBrainz dumps are rate-limit-free and complete.
- **Core connection rule: a connection is ONLY a shared recording** (2+ artists co-credited on the same track), and that recording is the connecting song. **No membership/relationship edges.** A band and its members are distinct nodes — never substitute one for the other. Every edge carries a playable song. (This reversed an earlier "expand band membership to member-member edges" idea after checking the data.)
- **Key nodes on MBID, not display name.**
- **Previews via iTunes (primary) + Deezer (fallback)**, query-time, Spotify-independent.
- **First iteration = depth 2 only.** Build small, learn the MusicBrainz infrastructure, expand later.
- **Do NOT discard Spotify data/crawler** — build MusicBrainz into a separate DB with a reversible config cutover.
- **Ingest a 4-table subset** (`artist`, `artist_credit`, `artist_credit_name`, `recording`), not a full Postgres mirror; `l_artist_artist` dropped since relationships aren't edges.
- **Producer/credits graph pinned as a separate future work tree** (candidate STRATEGY.md Track 5), not in this plan's scope.
- **Deployment stays deferred** to the existing depth-3/deploy plan, resumed after the graph is built.
